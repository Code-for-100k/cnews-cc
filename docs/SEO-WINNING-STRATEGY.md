## AGENT SCORECARDS

### Agent 1: Programmatic SEO Blitz -- SCORE: 8/10
**Best tactic**: Token converter pages (the Coinbase play). 600+ pages targeting transactional "CC to USD" queries with near-zero competition and proven page template. Instant keyword coverage at scale.
**What's wasteful**: 250 ecosystem entity pages at launch is overbuilt. Start with 50 high-search-volume entities, not all 250. The glossary (80-100 pages) is low-priority -- those terms have tiny volume.
**Verdict**: The volume engine. Converter pages + CIP pages are the fastest path to indexing hundreds of rankable URLs.

### Agent 2: Content Velocity Engine -- SCORE: 7/10
**Best tactic**: The 10-cluster topic hub architecture with pillar-spoke internal linking. This is what builds topical authority in Google's eyes. The first 15 articles (pillar pages) are perfectly sequenced.
**What's wasteful**: 8-12 articles per day is unrealistic quality-wise and unnecessary. The 3-layer Claude prompt system is overengineered for speed. The "Phase 4: Dominance" month 7-12 planning is irrelevant to 90-day goal. 720+ articles by month 6 risks Helpful Content penalties.
**Verdict**: Good architecture, needs ruthless scoping down. Publish 50 high-quality articles in 30 days, not 120 mediocre ones.

### Agent 3: Parasite SEO + Backlinks -- SCORE: 9/10
**Best tactic**: Parasite SEO on Medium/Substack/HackerNoon for DAY ONE SERP visibility. While cnews.cc is in Google sandbox, these DA 90+ platforms rank immediately. Combined with embeddable widgets for passive backlinks, this is the fastest authority builder.
**What's wasteful**: Wikipedia strategy (50+ credibility edits before touching Canton page = 3+ months of prep for a nofollow link). YouTube channel at launch. Telegram/Discord communities. Press releases at $1,500 each -- low ROI for SEO.
**Verdict**: The authority accelerator. Parasite SEO + widgets + validator outreach is the trifecta.

### Agent 4: Technical SEO Domination -- SCORE: 6/10
**Best tactic**: Rich schema markup on every page type (FAQ, HowTo, NewsArticle, FinancialProduct, Dataset, Event). Canton.network has ZERO structured data. Every SERP feature they can't capture, we steal. Featured snippet formatting rules (40-60 word paragraphs, numbered lists, comparison tables) are precise and actionable.
**What's wasteful**: 90% of this plan is table-stakes technical work (Core Web Vitals, caching headers, font loading) that should take 2-3 days max, not a whole strategy. The Event schema for governance votes is marginal. Over-optimizing bundle size below 80KB is diminishing returns.
**Verdict**: Important foundation but not a ranking differentiator. Do it once in Week 1, then forget about it. The schema markup + featured snippet formatting is the extractable value.

### Agent 5: Competitor Destruction -- SCORE: 7/10
**Best tactic**: The 4-tier keyword attack sequence (zero competition first, then low, then medium, then high). This prioritization is exactly right. Week 1-2 targeting 20+ keywords with ZERO dedicated pages means guaranteed #1 positions within days of indexing.
**What's wasteful**: "Skyscraper" strategy for 5,000-7,000 word guides is slow to produce and overkill for Week 1. Obsessing over canton.network's backlink profile when their content is the real weakness. The "defensive moat" section is month 6+ thinking, not month 1.
**Verdict**: Excellent target prioritization, mediocre execution plan. Take the keyword tiers, discard the rest.

Canton.network has zero structured data. Every cnews.cc page gets appropriate schema: Article/NewsArticle for blog posts, DefinedTerm for glossary, SoftwareApplication for ecosystem, WebApplication for tools, Organization for entities. **NOTE: FAQPage schema is RESTRICTED to government/healthcare sites only since Aug 2023 (per Google QRG). The single highest-ROI linkable asset. Compile on-chain data into a quarterly report that journalists, analysts, and ecosystem participants MUST cite. No one else produces this. **Publish in Week 2. Expect 20-40 citation backlinks.**

## WHAT TO SKIP

These tactics from the 5 plans sound impressive but are slow, expensive, or low-ROI for the 90-day speed objective.

### Skip: Wikipedia Editor Credibility Building (Agent 3)
50+ unrelated edits to build credibility before touching Canton page = 3 months minimum for a NOFOLLOW link. The ROI per hour is terrible. Do it eventually, but not in the first 90 days.

### Skip: YouTube Channel at Launch (Agent 3)
Video production is slow, expensive, and does not directly rank cnews.cc pages. Video SEO is a 6+ month play. Skip until Month 4.

### Skip: 5,000-7,000 Word Skyscraper Guides (Agent 5)
A single 7,000-word guide takes 2-3 days to produce well. In that time, you could publish 15 programmatic pages that each rank for different keywords. Write 2,500-3,500 word guides that are better than competitors (who average 1,500 words), not 3x longer.

### Skip: Press Releases via PRNewswire (Agent 3)
$1,500 per release for DA 20-60 syndication links with nofollow. The same effort spent on parasite SEO (free) and HARO responses (free) yields higher-DA dofollow links. Skip until Month 4+ when you have newsworthy milestones.

### Skip: Telegram/Discord Community Building (Agent 3)
Community building is a 6-12 month play. Zero direct SEO impact. Does not move rankings.

### Skip: Academic/University Outreach (Agent 3)
Extremely slow response cycle. .edu links are valuable but take 3-6 months to materialize. Queue for Month 4+.

### Skip: 720+ Articles by Month 6 (Agent 2)
This velocity risks triggering Google's Helpful Content penalty. Quality degrades at this scale even with AI assistance. 300-400 GOOD articles + 800 programmatic pages is the right mix.

### Skip: Glossary Pages for Obscure Terms (Agent 1)
"Command deduplication," "contract key," "party ID" -- these have near-zero search volume. Only build glossary pages for terms with 100+ monthly searches. That's roughly 30 terms, not 100.

### Skip: 175 Comparison Pages in First 90 Days (Agent 1)
"Alpend vs Aave" has essentially zero search volume. Build the 15-20 comparison pages with actual search demand (Canton vs top L1s, CC vs top tokens, Daml vs Solidity). Skip the rest.

### Skip: Overengineering Core Web Vitals Below Threshold (Agent 4)
LCP < 1.2s mobile, INP < 50ms, CLS < 0.02 -- these are gold-plated targets. Google's "good" thresholds are 2.5s, 200ms, 0.1 respectively. Hit "good" on Day 1, then stop optimizing. Spend that time publishing content.

## BOTTOM LINE

The winning formula is: **Programmatic scale (converter + entity + CIP pages) + editorial quality on high-volume keywords + parasite SEO for Day 1 visibility + widgets for passive backlinks + rich schema on everything (Article, DefinedTerm, SoftwareApplication — NOT FAQPage which is restricted).**

First blood comes from zero-competition CIP/validator/fees keywords in Week 1. The converter page engine floods Google with 600+ indexable pages in Week 2-3. Parasite SEO on Medium/Substack provides SERP coverage while cnews.cc builds domain authority. The "State of Canton" report becomes the single most-cited Canton data source.

By Day 90: 1,200+ pages indexed, 30-50 keywords in top 20, page 1 for 15-25 long-tail keywords, DA 25-35, and 5,000-15,000 organic sessions/month.
